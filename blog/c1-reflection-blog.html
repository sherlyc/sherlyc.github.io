Sprint 1: Reflections

What's your take on the DBC/EDA experience?

I'm quite happy I take the plunge to sign up with EDA, so far everything has been positive for me.

What are your impressions?

It's all good and I'm glad that I get to learn with a group of people who works toward a common goal. I could see where my fellow classmates are up to with the waffle board, this has been keeping me in check with my course work.

How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?

Well, I am pushing myself to participate in the group chat as much as I can, getting to know my fellow classmates, also trying to help as much as I can because I believe learning is best when you are teaching someone else.

Have your expectations of EDA changed? If so, how?

Not really. I have done my research before signing up so I'm glad that EDA is delivering what they promised.

Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous?

Yes, I'm a little nervous. I'm a shy person and hopefully by the end of this bootcamp, I will learn to express myself comfortably, develop active listening skill and becoming a good NZ cultural fit.
